== Runtime View

This section shows four representative flows that are architecturally relevant for the Platform Control Plane: authorization, inherited rules and guardrails, environment isolation, and one integrated governed deployment scenario.

Across these flows, owner-managed state follows the accepted propagation pattern from link:../adrs/0011-select-owner-managed-state-integration-and-projection-technology-profile.adoc[ADR 0011]: owner updates happen inside the owning capability boundary, the same change captures a transactional outbox entry or equivalent durable change record, and downstream projections plus cross-capability read models are updated asynchronously and can lag behind owner state.

=== Authorization Decision Flow

[mermaid, format=svg]
....
sequenceDiagram
  actor Requester
  participant IAM as Identity and Access
  participant Scope as Platform Hierarchy
  participant Policy as Policy
  participant Audit as Audit Records

  Requester->>IAM: 1. Resolve actor identity
  IAM->>Scope: 2. Resolve direct memberships, flat group memberships, and scoped grants
  Scope-->>IAM: Scope chain plus direct and group-derived grant context
  IAM->>IAM: 3. Expand roles and union permissions
  IAM->>IAM: 4. Default deny if required permission is absent
  IAM->>Policy: 5. Evaluate combined effective policy only when permission is present
  Policy-->>IAM: 6. Allow or deny decision
  IAM->>Audit: 7. Audit missing-permission denials, policy denials, and successful allows
  IAM-->>Requester: 8. Return allow or deny
....

. Resolve the requesting actor as either a user or a service account.
. Resolve direct memberships, flat group memberships, and scoped grants for tenant, workspace, workload, and environment.
. Expand the granted role set into fine-grained permissions and union direct and group-derived permissions.
. Deny by default when the required permission is absent at the requested scope or any ancestor scope.
. Evaluate the combined effective policy only after permission lookup succeeds.
. Return an allow or deny decision and audit missing-permission denials, policy denials, and successful allows.

When a decision requires the freshest membership, grant, or policy data, the responsible flow reads from the owning capability rather than relying on a projected read model.

=== Inherited Rules and Effective Policy Evaluation

[mermaid, format=svg]
....
flowchart TD
  PlatformBaseline["Platform Policy Baseline"] --> TenantPolicy["Tenant Policy"]
  TenantPolicy --> WorkspacePolicy["Workspace Policy"]
  WorkspacePolicy --> WorkloadPolicy["Workload Policy"]
  WorkloadPolicy --> EnvironmentPolicy["Environment Policy"]
  EnvironmentPolicy --> EffectivePolicy["Combined Effective Policy for Requested Action"]
  Mandatory["Mandatory parent controls cannot be weakened"] --> EffectivePolicy
  Tightening["Child scopes may tighten controls"] --> EffectivePolicy
....

. Start with the platform policy baseline.
. Apply tenant, workspace, workload, and environment policy in hierarchy order.
. Preserve mandatory parent controls throughout evaluation.
. Allow lower scopes to tighten controls, such as requiring approval or restricting production access to a narrower group.
. Use the resulting combined effective policy during authorization and execution decisions.

Derived policy-aware read models may assist downstream consumers, but Policy remains the official record for policy state and downstream projections may lag.

=== Environment Isolation Resolution

[mermaid, format=svg]
....
flowchart TD
  Subscription["Subscription"] --> Plan["Subscription Plan"]
  Subscription --> IsolationProfile["Isolation Profile"]
  Environment["Environment"] --> IsolationProfile
  IsolationProfile --> EnvironmentPlacement["Environment Placement"]
  IsolationProfile --> DataBoundary["Data Boundary"]
  IsolationProfile --> NetworkBoundary["Network Boundary"]
  Environment --> Tightening["Environment-Level Tightening"]
  Tightening --> EnvironmentPlacement
  Tightening --> DataBoundary
  Tightening --> NetworkBoundary
  Policy["Combined Effective Policy"] --> EnvironmentPlacement
  Policy --> DataBoundary
  Policy --> NetworkBoundary
  EnvironmentPlacement --> Placement["Resolved Placement"]
  DataBoundary --> Placement
  NetworkBoundary --> Placement
  Placement --> AcceptDeny["Placement Accepted or Denied"]
....

. Resolve the environment and its parent subscription context.
. Read the subscription's subscription plan and isolation profile as the default isolation baseline.
. Apply any environment-level tightening that strengthens the default without weakening it.
. Evaluate policy constraints that can affect placement, region, network, retention, or access posture.
. Resolve environment placement, data boundary, and network boundary independently under one policy envelope.
. Accept placement only when every resolved boundary complies with policy and does not weaken a stricter default.
. Produce the effective isolation outcome for the environment without changing the business hierarchy.

Runtime and placement state are committed by the owning capability first, recorded with a durable change record, and then propagated to downstream consumers or projections through asynchronous reconciliation as needed.

=== Canonical Governed Deployment Scenario

[mermaid, format=svg]
....
sequenceDiagram
  actor Automation as Service Account
  participant IAM as Identity and Access
  participant Hierarchy as Platform Hierarchy
  participant Policy as Policy
  participant Isolation as Environment Isolation
  participant Audit as Audit Records

  Automation->>IAM: 1. Present short-lived bearer token for production deployment
  IAM->>Hierarchy: 2. Resolve environment scope, workload, workspace, and tenant context
  Hierarchy-->>IAM: 3. Return scope chain plus scoped grants
  IAM->>IAM: 4. Confirm `environment.deploy` at the target production environment
  IAM->>Policy: 5. Submit actor, scope, approval reference, region, and execution-context inputs
  Policy->>Policy: 6. Validate pre-approved deployment request, approved region, and trusted execution context
  Policy->>Isolation: 7. Allow production placement evaluation under hybrid isolation rules
  Isolation->>Isolation: 8. Resolve dedicated runtime unit, data boundary, and network boundary
  Isolation->>Audit: 9. Record placement outcome and resulting boundary decision
  Policy->>Audit: 10. Record policy outcome and approval reference
  IAM->>Audit: 11. Record successful deployment authorization context
  IAM-->>Automation: 12. Return approved deployment execution outcome
....

. A service account presents a short-lived bearer token and requests `environment.deploy` for a production environment inside an existing workload of a hybrid tenant.
. Identity and Access resolves the actor, reads the freshest scope and grant context from Platform Hierarchy, and confirms that the service account holds the required scoped `environment.deploy` permission for the target environment.
. Policy evaluates the deployment against the combined effective policy and validates the referenced pre-approved deployment request, the requested region, and the trusted network or execution context.
. Because the request satisfies the governing rules, Environment Isolation resolves the production environment to the required dedicated runtime unit and the corresponding dedicated boundaries for that environment under the tenant's hybrid isolation profile.
. Audit Records preserve the actor identity, scope path, requested permission, approval reference, policy outcome, and final environment placement outcome so the deployment can be reviewed later.
. The owner-first commit and durable downstream propagation still follow link:../adrs/0011-select-owner-managed-state-integration-and-projection-technology-profile.adoc[ADR 0011], so owner-managed state is committed before dependent projections are refreshed.

If the same deployment request lacks a valid approval reference, targets a region that policy does not allow, or would weaken a stricter default boundary, the platform denies the request. That denial is audited with the actor, scope, requested action, and denial reason, and no environment placement change is committed.
